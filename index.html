About

“

Digital business and entrepreneurship is, by design, a broad degree. That breadth suits me. What I keep coming back to are the questions that live at the edge of fields — where business logic meets how people actually think, or where a technology shifts what is even worth paying attention to in the first place.

Getting into HTML and CSS was curiosity more than obligation. I wanted to understand how things on a screen are actually built. The coding itself was useful, but the bigger shift was noticing how much cleaner your thinking about systems becomes once you have tried to construct one yourself, even a small one. That change in perspective has lasted longer than the skill.

The business side is where I feel most grounded. Not strategy as a neat concept, but the more specific question of what actually makes something work, and what you would see differently if you came at it from a less obvious angle. That instinct is what learning how to learn really points toward.
